# Plot Overview

The game is structured in four parts named for the four stages of the alchemical **Magnum Opus** — see [themes.md](themes.md). The story is meant to parallel that arc spiritually as well as literally.

## Premise

The world is recovering from the collapse of the ancient **Hermetic Empire**, which fell when its god-like alchemists — wielders of the **Philosopher's Stone** — lost their power overnight. Today, "alchemy" is a hereditary talent: only those born with an affinity can channel **runes** to bend the elements. Two great powers have risen: **[Paracelsus](world/factions.md#paracelsus)**, the classist alchemist oligarchy, and the **[Coalition Pax Facticius (CPF)](world/factions.md#coalition-pax-facticius-cpf)**, a league of mechanist city-states that answered alchemical supremacy with industry and prosthetics. Pulling strings beneath both is **[Ouroboros](world/factions.md#ouroboros)**, a shadow society hunting the secret of the Stone — and, older than all of it, **[the Philosopher](characters/npcs/philosopher.md)**, the Stone's original creator, who has spent centuries ensuring it can never be made again.

You follow **[the Bleeding Hearts](world/factions.md#the-bleeding-hearts)**, a border mercenary band that takes in outcasts — and that stumbles into the center of all of it because one of its newest recruits, **[Isaac](characters/isaac.md)**, carries the long-extinct **alkahest** affinity: the key to recreating the Stone.

## Part 1: Melanosis (the blackening)

*Trial, loss, and corruption. The party is forged out of catastrophe.* (See [mission-flow.md](mission-flow.md) for the level breakdown.)

- Orphans **Isaac** and **Rebecca** arrive at the **Paracelsus Military Academy** to enlist — their only escape from years of poverty since their parents were murdered. (Why now? They've both finally hit the minimum enlistment age.)
- Testing reveals **Rebecca** has no affinity, while **Isaac** can use *every* base rune — impossible for a normal alchemist, and shocking to him because his parents had always sworn neither sibling had any affinity. He feels betrayed; the instructors are intrigued enough to admit him. 🔴 *Who first grasps the significance of Isaac's ability is unsettled* → [Q3](appendix/open-questions.md#q3-who-first-identifies-isaacs-alkahest).
- The academy is raided by hired mercenary gangs. In the chaos, **Isaac** and the aether-healer **[Aldin](characters/aldin.md)** search for Rebecca — and find her cornered, her arm severed by an attacker.
- One mercenary, **[Noemie](characters/noemie.md)**, turns on her own side in disgust, helps them escape, and offers to take the wounded Rebecca to the border, where mechanist technology can fit her with a prosthetic arm — for a price owed to her boss, **[Torv](characters/torv.md)**.
- At the Bleeding Hearts' hideout, Torv sizes up the newcomers (an impossible alchemist, a healer, and a fighter-to-be) and strikes a deal: he'll outfit Rebecca's arm if Isaac runs a job — sacking a **CPF** transport carrying exactly that kind of prosthetic material.
- The job succeeds; Rebecca is armed and the three formally join. Then the hideout is itself raided by the spurned gangs. Torv crushes them personally, but now he's certain something is wrong: gangs attacking a military academy *and* his own camp is no coincidence. He sends a small group to tail the other mercenaries.
- They witness — and interrupt — a **kidnapping**, rescuing **[Prince Cid](characters/cid.md)** of the CPF. Returning him to **[King Campeador](characters/npcs/king-campeador.md)** earns the Bleeding Hearts a **Letter of Marque**... and a "mission" aboard a royal airship.
- The airship is a trap. 🔴 *The king's true plan and Ouroboros's role* → [Q1](appendix/open-questions.md#q1-ouroboros-the-acolytes-and-the-cartel). Campeador, secretly behind the border raids, means to sabotage the flight, frame Paracelsus, and spark a war — disposing of the troublesome mercenaries and a shipload of pro-peace diplomats at once. But Cid stows away. To force the war, a high-ranking **Ouroboros** agent who can *control dragons* personally downs the airship over a **secret dragon-experimentation lab**, and Ouroboros mercenaries encircle the wreck to leave no survivors — just as the king's own men arrive to save Cid.
- **Part 1 boss:** a three-way battle among the Ouroboros mercenaries, the king's forces, and the Bleeding Hearts — capped by a **dragon**.

## Part 2: Leucosis (the whitening)

*Purification: hunting a crime syndicate out of the shadows.*

- The survivors regroup, saving a **diplomat** from the wreckage who joins them to "uncover the truth." 🔴 **He is secretly an Ouroboros agent** feeding them steered information → [Q1](appendix/open-questions.md#q1-ouroboros-the-acolytes-and-the-cartel).
- Torv, unable to simply attack a whole nation, targets the rival mercenary group that was waiting at the crash. The trail leads to **[the Cartel](world/factions.md#the-cartel)** 🏷 — a human-trafficking operation harvesting the dying's aether to forge **fake Philosopher's Stones**. Somewhere here the party learns what really happened to **[Isaac and Rebecca's parents](characters/npcs/dead-parents.md)**, making it personal.
- *Behind the scenes:* the Cartel has outlived its usefulness to Ouroboros, who steer the Bleeding Hearts into destroying it — hoping to weaken both at once.
- The party fights to the **Cartel Leader**, who has seized a powerful alkahest-bearing stone and turned on his Ouroboros handlers. 🔴 *Exact choreography of his death and what intervenes is a known fork* → [Q1](appendix/open-questions.md#q1-ouroboros-the-acolytes-and-the-cartel). The favored version: after a hard fight, a **second** fight erupts as Ouroboros (and/or the Philosopher's Acolytes) move in to finish everyone — staged as an **underground cave collapse** the party must escape alive, using earth alchemy to clear routes. The diplomat "heroically sacrifices himself" — a ploy to slip back to Ouroboros while the party believes him dead.

## Part 3: Xanthosis (the yellowing)

*From serving oneself to serving others; the truth of the world opens up.*

- Torv realizes the whole Cartel hunt was a setup and that bad information has been steered to them. Using intelligence the Bleeding Hearts gathered but *weren't supposed to*, they uncover the **dragon facilities in the wilderness** and the first real shreds of evidence about **Ouroboros** — including proof that it was **not** Paracelsus that downed the airship (this is a *Paracelsus* dragon facility, and they didn't do it).
- This is the worldbuilding-delivery chapter: the dragons tie back to the ancient cataclysm, grounding the Philosopher as a character before he appears. 🏷 *The cataclysm still needs a name.*
- The dragon base is run by **[Torv's Sister](characters/npcs/torvs-sister.md)** — high-class Paracelsus (not Ouroboros), the first foe to make Torv falter; possibly the one who took his leg and drove him from the wilderness years ago. The CPF, tracking the dragon that downed their airship, may converge here too, throwing **mechs** into the mix.
- **Part 3 boss:** a massive dragon battle at the facility.

## Part 4: Iosis (the reddening)

*Enlightenment; the confrontation with both "Magnum Opi."* — **the most fluid part.**

Threads to settle, each a candidate finale beat:
- **King Campeador** — his pursuit of immortality culminating in a **full cyborg body** (the mechanist "Magnum Opus" — the fully autonomous human).
- **The Alchemic Council of Five** — crazy high-end alchemy fights: huge runestone amplifiers, the dammed river through the capital, the great Library.
- **Ouroboros and the Philosopher** — the true endgame. 🔴 *The Philosopher's modern power level and goal are a major reframe* → [Q2](appendix/open-questions.md#q2-the-philosophers-power-and-goal). The reframed direction: the Philosopher is **weakened**, secretly behind the Cartel, and wants **Isaac as the second alkahest alchemist** required to forge a *true* new Stone — manipulating the whole story to "train" him.

**The throughline to preserve:** the Bleeding Hearts keep getting thrown into impossible fights because **Ouroboros engineers its enemies into destroying one another**, intending to step in only at the very end — when one weakened group remains, guard down.

### Candidate endings (open)
- The Philosopher kills Isaac believing he's extinguished alkahest (and thus alchemy) forever — final scene: Isaac's child being born.
- The Philosopher is beaten, but all rune-alchemy stops working because every stone was tied to his life — yet Isaac, the last alkahest line, leaves a seed for alchemy to bloom anew without Stones.
