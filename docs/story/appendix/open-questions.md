## Q1: Ouroboros, the Acolytes, and the Cartel

**The question:** What is the actual control structure among the three shadow players — and are **Ouroboros** and the **Philosopher's Acolytes** the same thing, two opposed groups, or parent-and-splinter?

**What's consistent across docs:**
- The **Cartel** is *muscle / a fake-stone factory*, not a prime mover — someone pulls its strings.
- **Ouroboros** wants the **Stone / power / immortality** and works by **manipulation**, not direct force.
- The **Acolytes** follow the **Philosopher**, whose stated creed is to **prevent** the Stone.

**Where they disagree — who controls the Cartel:**
| Version | Source |
|---|---|
| **Ouroboros** puppets the Cartel (taught it fake stones; steers the Bleeding Hearts into destroying it once it's spent) | `The Cartel`, `Small Cartel re-write` |
| **The Philosopher** is *secretly behind* the Cartel (he's trying to gather power for a real Stone) | `Absolute Power Corrupts Absolutely` (the reframe) |

**The deeper structural fork — what *is* Ouroboros:**
- **(a) A defector splinter of the Philosopher's cult** that broke away *wanting* the Stone — i.e. Ouroboros and the Acolytes are **opposed**, with opposite goals. (`Moderately Long Narrative Imperative`: *"Secret society (because defect from Philosopher's group? wants immortality?), … specifically OUROBOROS."*)
- **(b) Effectively merged** under the reframe — if the Philosopher himself now wants a Stone and backs the Cartel, the line between "anti-Stone Acolytes" and "pro-Stone Ouroboros" blurs.

**Front-runner:** the richest, most recent material (the reframe + `Small Cartel re-write`) points to a **three-body** arrangement — Acolytes (the Philosopher's true believers), Ouroboros (the pro-Stone manipulators), and the Cartel (disposable muscle) — that **collide** at the Cartel Leader's death, with the diplomat-agent escaping with the alkahest stone. But whether the Philosopher and Ouroboros are **allies, rivals, or the same hand** is exactly what's unresolved.

**Knock-on decisions:** the Part-2 boss choreography (single fight vs. boss-then-Ouroboros vs. boss-then-cave-collapse), who the "diplomat" answers to, and whether the Acolytes first appear *hunting the same stone* in Part 2 or later.

## Q2: The Philosopher's Power and Goal

**The question:** Is the Philosopher the **all-powerful absolutist** of his character bio, or the **weakened, corrupting schemer** of the reframe? They can't both be the live version.

| | **Version A — the bio** (`The Philosopher`) | **Version B — the reframe** (`Absolute Power Corrupts Absolutely`) |
|---|---|---|
| **Power now** | God-tier; wiped out *all* alkahest alchemists single-handedly | **Weakened** by the ancient battle; his Stone may now do nothing but keep his ancient body intact (lich/phylactery weak point) |
| **Goal** | Forever **prevent** any new Stone; tragic Sisyphean exterminator | Slid into a **god-complex**; secretly **wants a new Stone** and to be humanity's invisible hand |
| **Cartel** | (not his) | **He's behind it**, gathering power |
| **Isaac** | a bloodline to **eliminate** | the **second alkahest alchemist** he needs (a true Stone requires two); manipulates events to "train" him |
| **His cult** | believes truly; he is what he seems | **less powerful than it preaches**; only the top (if anyone) knows he's weakened; cracks show when the Bleeding Hearts prove the Acolytes beatable |

**Why the reframe exists (dev's own reasoning):** Version A makes him **unbeatable** (how do the heroes ever win?) and makes the world's ongoing chaos depend on him *choosing not to intervene*, which strains belief for a set-up "wise man." Version B fixes both, **humanizes the cult**, explains why Isaac/Rebecca exist (he could only do so much before falling from power — not an oversight), and **gives the story an actual destination/finale**.

**Front-runner:** **Version B** — it's the later, more developed thinking and the dev explicitly built it to solve A's problems. If adopted, **the Philosopher's bio needs rewriting** to match (power level, motive, relationship to the Cartel and to Isaac). Treated as *not yet ratified* pending the co-dev talk.

**Riders bundled with B:**
- A true Stone needs **two** alkahest alchemists (already in `The Philosopher's stone` lore). → makes Isaac indispensable rather than disposable.
- Did the Philosopher **spare a pregnant relative** long ago (hidden from his own cult), making **Isaac the deliberately-spared last bloodline**? (`Moderately Long Narrative Imperative`, `Dead Parents`.)
- Endings hinge on this — see [plot-overview § candidate endings](../plot-overview.md#candidate-endings-open).

## Q3: Who First Identifies Isaac's Alkahest?

**The question:** Three docs each imply a different "first to know," and they can't all be first.

| Claimant | How | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **The academy / Ouroboros's informant** | The test exposes Isaac using all elements; the info is forwarded to Ouroboros, whose agent is "the one to recognize Isaac's Alkahest first" | `Chapter 1`, `Ouroboros Agents` |
| **Torv** | Raised in Paracelsus, he spots the runic anomaly **very early** and **says nothing** (reacting would be suspicious) | `Torv` bio |
| **Sophia** | The scholar who **first connects** Isaac's ability to alkahest theory | `Sophia` bio — *which carries a margin note disputing itself:* "Umm I thought Torv notices really early on…" |

**Note these aren't fully exclusive:** "notices an anomaly," "names it as *alkahest*," and "tells the player/party" are **three different beats** and could be split across Torv (silent early notice), the Ouroboros agent (names it, off-screen, for the villains), and Sophia (names it *to the party*, on-screen). Cleanest fix is probably to **assign each beat deliberately** rather than pick one winner.

## Q9: The Rebecca Reveal (proposed beat, 2026-07-08)

**The proposal (dev, via scratchpad):** flip the academy opening's test order so the affinity test *makes* Rebecca's tragedy instead of just reporting it:

1. **Isaac tests first — and shows every affinity.** While the staff are still confirming the impossible result, and **before Rebecca can test**, the attack on the camp hits.
2. In the raid she loses her arm (canon), and **the maim's aura tax** (−1 point, highest pool first — [alchemy-kit.md](../../design/alchemy-kit.md) aura model) **zeroes her out** — because Rebecca, Isaac's sister by blood, actually carries **a single hidden alkahest point** just like him. Nobody ever knew: alkahest presents as affinity-for-everything, and she never got her test.
3. The world's answer — and hers — becomes "no affinity," and it's now *mechanically true* (the transmutation doctrine's **Rebecca rule**, 0 aura = nothing, already carries her name). The real story: she was one point from being her brother's mirror.

**Why it's attractive:** welds her backstory to the aura/limb-tax mechanics (her loss is systemic, not narrated); deepens the Stone wish ("gain an affinity" — regrowth restores taxed points, so the wish has a mechanical door); plants a late reveal that recolors her entire arc.

**The flagged problem (dev):** the same mechanics point back at Isaac — if the player takes him down the **maim route in story before he trains any element**, his highest (only) pool is the hidden alkahest point; the −1 tax would zero the protagonist's whole kit (universal breadth rides alkahest — audit A6). Wants a rule before the beat is canon: is the alkahest point untaxable? Is pre-training Isaac shielded from maim by story structure? Or does the tax take *grown* points only, never born ones — in which case Rebecca's zero-out needs restating (her point born, or the rule asymmetric). **Story call for the co-dev; the mechanics ruling lands in [alchemy-kit.md](../../design/alchemy-kit.md).**
