# King Campeador

🟡 **Draft canon** · `Story/Characters/NPCs/King Campeador.docx` · *(spelled "Compeador" in the CPF doc)*
**Role:** antagonist · **Faction:** ruler of the [CPF](../../world/factions.md#coalition-pax-facticius-cpf) · **Family:** father of [Cid](../cid.md)

The ruler of the **Coalition Pax Facticius** — beloved by his citizens and meticulous about his public image. He can be described as **Machiavellian**: he rules by cunning and manipulation while maintaining the face of a benevolent, sympathetic ruler. His **one and only fear is death** — his own or his loved ones' — and to defeat it he will sacrifice any number of his people and make enemies of other nations, believing immortality is an end that justifies any means.

## The wound that drives him

He cherished his **wife**, who bore him one son, **[Cid](../cid.md)**, and was devastated when she **died of an incurable disease** — instilling a deep terror of mortality. In her wake he funded medical research using state-of-the-art technology, out of which came functional **prosthetics** — but no cure. When he learned **he too had the disease**, his search turned desperate, down two leads:

1. **Full transfer of the self into a mechanical vessel** — immortality free of the flesh. He knew the public would never accept it and wasn't keen to surrender his humanity, so he pursued it **in secret**. *(This is the "cyborg King" finale thread — see [plot-overview Part 4](../../plot-overview.md#part-4-iosis-the-reddening).)*
2. **The Philosopher's Stone.** He met the Paracelsus **Council of Five**, who told him the Stone was a myth — but an **[Ouroboros](../../world/factions.md#ouroboros)** contact revealed that someone in Paracelsus *did* know how to make one, as a closely guarded secret. Feeling **slighted and betrayed**, he resolved to **force Paracelsus to show its hand**: invade with a force so overwhelming they'd have to use the Stone to survive — and reveal it to him.

## The scheme

Knowing his people wouldn't accept an unprovoked war, he arranged **covert mercenary operations** to both irritate Paracelsus and manufacture a pretext — culminating in the **staged kidnapping of his own son**, to be blamed on Paracelsus. He **never meant for Cid to be harmed**, and didn't let him in on the plan (Cid would never have gone along with the deception). The Bleeding Hearts' rescue of Cid made the "kidnapping" look real.

To remove the mercenaries before they could investigate who hired the kidnappers, he hired the whole group to **defend "precious cargo" aboard an airship** — secretly routed over **Paracelsus's secret dragon grounds** (intel from his Ouroboros agent). He sold it as easy work but wanted them all aboard. **Cid stows away**; by the time the King realizes, it's too late to recall the ship, so he sends his Ouroboros agent and trusted men to save his son and finish the mercenaries — the moment the party (and Cid) become **certain the King is hiding something**. → [Q1](../../appendix/open-questions.md#q1-ouroboros-the-acolytes-and-the-cartel) for Ouroboros's parallel agenda here.
