# Torv

🟡 **Draft canon** · `Story/Characters/Playable/Torv.docx`
**Role:** leader of the [Bleeding Hearts](../world/factions.md#the-bleeding-hearts) · **Weapon:** kinetic mace · **Prosthetic:** leg · **Origin:** high-born Paracelsus, **no affinity**

The leader of the mercenary group — built like a rock, wielding a **kinetic mace**, far more skilled in combat than any of his people. A good judge of character and a tough, effective leader with a deep sense of **guardianship**: he takes it on himself to shield others from injustice.

Unlike mercenaries who chose the life for money or glory, the **Bleeding Hearts formed around Torv as a refuge** for those with nowhere else to go — the poor, the persecuted, anyone useful he deems honest enough. He takes only jobs he believes are just, even when that's impractical money-wise. Because he was **ostracized in Paracelsus for lacking alchemical ability**, he is especially protective of those marginalized for things they couldn't control.

**If Torv had the Philosopher's Stone:** he would use it to **fund the retirement of all his men** and help refugees and outcasts get back on their feet.

## Role in Part 1

Torv sees a job going around to attack the [Paracelsus Military Academy](../locations/paracelsus.md). The other gangs are barbaric and kill for pay (and for pleasure); Torv smells something wrong and sends only a **few** people — including [Noemie](noemie.md) — to watch what happens. They save Isaac, Aldin, and Rebecca, though not before Rebecca's arm is lost.

Reading [Isaac](isaac.md)'s peculiar affinity, [Aldin](aldin.md)'s aether healing, and [Rebecca](rebecca.md)'s combat potential, Torv agrees to outfit Rebecca's arm **if Isaac runs a job** — and, lacking any spare prosthetics, sends him to sack a **CPF transport** he knows is carrying exactly that material. It's a gamble he bets on Isaac's character to win, and it pays off: extra bounty and new fighters. When his own camp is raided soon after, he arms Rebecca, leads the defense personally, and crushes the attackers as a show of force — then has Isaac and a few men **tail** the other gangs. When they return with **Prince [Cid](cid.md)**, Torv realizes he may have bitten off more than even he can chew.

## Backstory

Born to a **high-class Paracelsus family** but — rare there — **without an affinity**: a disappointment to his parents, a shame to his family, a reject of society. At **16** he ran away and survived **alone in the wilderness for five years**, building his immense strength and skill.

About **14 years before the game**, he came out of the wilderness with a crude, self-made **prosthetic leg**, needing a proper one. What took his leg is a mystery he won't speak of — rumor says he got too close to the **dragon grounds**. The truth: he stumbled onto one of the **aether-alchemist dragon-experimentation sites**, where half-tamed, twisted-minded dragons nearly killed him; he barely escaped even with all his training. He refuses to return to the wilderness until he believes he's strong enough to **stop the experimentation on the dragons** — so the party encounters it repeatedly.

Afterward he drifted the borderlands, joining and leaving mercenary groups, picking up the **kinetic mace** and learning how people on the margins of both nations actually live. Appalled (having been raised among the elite), he eventually founded his **own** group to take jobs that raise the standard of living in the slums while giving outcasts like himself a decent living. Nobody knows his upbringing, but it slips through — a too-insightful comment, precise knowledge of how runes work, a word too sophisticated for a hardened mercenary.
