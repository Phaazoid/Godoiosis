## Terrain & scenario hooks
- **Cliffs / winding road scaling upward / plateau** — air alchemists and mace-users can **push people off the side.**
- **A river you must bridge** (terrain modification) — and then **destroy the bridge to drown pursuers.**
- **Choke points you barricade** with terrain modification.
- **Defend something from waves** of enemies, or **advance through a maze**.
- **Great Wall of Paracelsus** 🏷 — a wall of **charged runestone** usable as an **artillery weapon.**

## Set-piece boss/level concepts *(from `Unorganized Threads`)*
- **Airship level** — the battlefield *is* a giant airship the units pilot; it gets **boarded**; units can man guns or dismount to fight boarders. (At least one "holding" map + one "boarding" map.)
- **Train level** — trains are steampunk; use terrain modification to **slow the train** to keep up, **board** it, beat the enemies inside, maybe **steal cargo** (the train has guns).
- **Caterpillar tank boss** — a mechanist creation (or alchemical chimera) that **occupies multiple tiles** and crawls like the *Centipede* enemy; segments have different abilities/weaknesses; enveloping a unit triggers a near-instakill constriction. *What happens if you destroy a middle segment — split or contract?*
- **Cave-collapse escape** — the Part 2 Cartel-stronghold finale: get everyone out alive while a too-strong enemy brings the ceiling down (falling rocks damage/instakill; earth alchemy clears routes; checkpoint after the boss).

> These connect to mechanics already in or planned for the engine (terrain modification, multi-tile units, formations). Cross-reference with [terrain.md](../../design/terrain.md) when designing.
