# Paracelsus

🟡 **Draft canon** · `Story/Locations/Paracelsus/Paracelsus.docx`
**Type:** nation + capital city · **The alchemist power.** Polity/government: [world/factions.md § Paracelsus](../world/factions.md#paracelsus).

The **alchemic capital of the known world** — founded and run by alchemists, with an extremely **classist** society based on family status and individual alchemical prowess.

## The capital city 🏷 *(unnamed; candidate "New Hermes")*

A massive city built like a **fortress**, home to most of the prominent figures of society. Inside the walls, order is near-perfect — almost no crime or poverty, everything gleaming, with **refined, sleek buildings and an elegant, orderly layout** that was planned and then executed easily through alchemical means. But the government's concern stops at the walls: poverty and crime are pushed outward, and the standard of living **drops the farther you travel from the capital**.

Defining features:
- Built over the **ruins of the ancient city** where alchemic power originates — the only place connected to the lost art of ancient alchemy, and thus considered **sacred**.
- The city center sits on the **largest preserved ancient runes**, and holds a great **Library/archive** of all runes and alchemy — a prestigious building open only to scholars and the elite.
- **Rune mines run directly beneath the capital** (it's built over the rune deposit). Mining proceeds while alchemically erecting supports to keep the ground stable — but a **large-scale battle in the mines could collapse parts of the city above.** A finale set-piece candidate.
- A **river runs through the city**; it can be **dammed and released** for mayhem (see [Mechanist–Alchemist duality](../themes.md)).
- Even this jewel has poverty on its outskirts — which the privileged **pretend doesn't exist** and try to purge as "vermin."

## Governance

An **oligarchy: the Council of Five**, the single most powerful alchemist of each element. They view the capital as the country's "true face" and neglect everything beyond it. Their fatal blind spot is their **ignorance of alkahest** and their **hubris**.

## Battle set-pieces here
The **rune mine**, the **Library** ("avec lazorz"), **runestone amplifiers**, the dammed **river**, and the **Great Wall of Paracelsus** 🏷 — a wall of charged runestone usable as an **artillery weapon**. Full list: [battle-locales.md](battle-locales.md).
