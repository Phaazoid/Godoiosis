# The Coalition Pax Facticius (CPF)

🟡 **Draft canon** · `Story/Locations/CPF/Coalition Pax Facticius.docx`
**Type:** nation (league of city-states) · **The mechanist power.** Polity/government: [world/factions.md § CPF](../world/factions.md#coalition-pax-facticius-cpf).

A **coalition of city-states** — "Factitious Peace." Each keeps its own identity but recognizes **[King Campeador](../characters/npcs/king-campeador.md)**, whose line descends from the largest founding state 🏷 that first united the Coalition. The CPF exists to **balance Paracelsus**, which before the Coalition had unchecked military supremacy.

Values are the inverse of Paracelsus's: **alchemical ability isn't a measure of standing.** The CPF empowers its citizens, workforce, and military through **technology**, which sparked a massive **industrial revolution** and an arms race to match elite alchemists. It's not openly antagonistic toward Paracelsus, but is **wary and resentful** of being looked down on; it wants parity, not war.

## Why there's so much to scavenge (backstory)

The CPF began **after** alchemy was rediscovered (you can't define "non-alchemist" until alchemy exists), formed by smaller nations rallying not for conquest but for **safety and balance**. It grew as an **unplanned sprawl** — people moved rather than planned, so some areas became dense and badly designed. When industry began producing **waste**, the cities were already packed and those in power treated disposal as secondary to advancement, so no real infrastructure developed. Eventually leaders designated a massive dumping ground — the **Scrap Heaps** — and "send it to the heaps" became reflexive in cities that increasingly **buy** technology rather than build it. Ironically, many store-bought spare parts were **scavenged from the heaps** to begin with, sometimes returning to the very shops that discarded them.

Real innovation is monopolized by a **small caste of high-status scientists** (who build the **war-machines/mechs** the party fights later). That secrecy has bred **political corruption** and deliberately-cultivated **technological ignorance** in the big cities — to keep the inquisitive from discovering what's really going on. The only people untouched by that ignorance are the **scavengers** who build to survive.
