## Alkahest — the base element

**Alkahest** is the universal element / *prima materia* — the chaotic, unrefined state of which all other elements (earth, air, fire, water, aether) are derivative forms. It is associated with **chaos**, and alkahest-based alchemy is considered by some groups **taboo, akin to dark magic**.

The real danger isn't the taboo — it's that creating a Stone requires handling **pure, unrefined alkahest, a universal solvent** so dangerous that careless handling "could endanger all life on the planet." Only someone with an **alkahest affinity** can safely *bend* pure alkahest. This affinity is **hereditary**, which is what made the Philosopher's purge so total (and so monstrous — he had to kill his own bloodline). It is all but extinct by the modern era; **Isaac** carries it.

> Alkahest doubles as the game's central **metaphor for runaway power / the Singularity** — see [themes.md](../themes.md).

## Runestone — how modern alchemy works

**Runestone is the "radioactive" aftermath of the violent dissolution of all the Philosopher's Stones.** When the Stones were destroyed, their power didn't simply vanish — over centuries it became **imbued into the ground itself** (the ancient ruins, and anywhere a Stone was destroyed), leaving a far weaker catalyst behind.

Three ingredients are needed to work a rune:
1. a **person with an affinity**,
2. an **alkahest-infused stone** (runestone), and
3. an **engraving** of an elemental symbol.

**Decided model (Theory 2):** the **person is the impetus**, the **stone is the power source**, and the **engraving unlocks** it. (This was chosen over "the person is the power source" because it cleanly explains why **runestone is exhaustible** while people with affinities are not — and avoids clashing with **aether**, which already represents life-force.)

- **Unengraved** runestone + an affinity → the latent alkahest releases *without* geometric resonance: weak, unpredictable effects (a color change, an odd smell — the proto-chemistry real alchemists fumbled with).
- The power is best channeled by **geometric symbols that resonate with an element**. The in-world discovery story: ancient alchemists performed stronger transmutations near ruins whose buildings were *laid out* in elemental symbols — and only later realized it was the **geometry, not the location**, that mattered, making alchemy portable by **engraving** the runes into stone. *(Fun framing: a dude meditating on shaped lines makes the first consistent transmutation and thinks he's a genius — but it was just the lines. A third-party "ancient diaries" device could deliver this.)*

This is why **Paracelsus** rose where it did — the largest runestone deposit (from the great battle, [timeline.md](../timeline.md)) sits beneath its capital — and why the **runestone running low** (Period 4) is a quietly existential threat.

## The Philosopher's Stone

The **Magnum Opus** of all alchemy — a stable manifestation of **alkahest**. It can transmute any element (lead to gold), heal any wound, and with consistent use grant **immortality** (the "Elixir of Life" half of its purpose). Possession made the Hermetic rulers god-like, and that power corrupted them — which is what turned the Philosopher against his own creation.

**True Stones vs. fakes — a deliberate FMA-style red herring:**
- Early on it's *assumed* a Stone can be made by **sacrificing human life**. This is **misinformation**, seeded in-world by **[the Cartel](factions.md#the-cartel)**'s (secretly Ouroboros/Philosopher-run) experiments, which only ever produce **crude imitations** doomed to fail.
- The **true** creation process requires a **joint transmutation by more than one alkahest-affine alchemist**. 🔴 This is the mechanical hinge of the reframed endgame — the Philosopher needing **Isaac as a second alchemist** → [Q2](../appendix/open-questions.md#q2-the-philosophers-power-and-goal).
- 🔴 One reframe also posits the Philosopher's own Stone can no longer do anything *but* keep his ancient body from crumbling — a lich-and-phylactery weak point. → [Q2](../appendix/open-questions.md#q2-the-philosophers-power-and-goal).

## Aether — note

**Aether** is the fifth element (the Philosopher's own discovery) and represents **life-force**: living things emit it when they die, which is the grisly basis for the Cartel's fake-stone process. Aether alchemy is the domain of **healers** like [Aldin](../characters/aldin.md), and is entangled with the **dragon** experiments in the wilderness ([locations/wilderness.md](../locations/wilderness.md)).
