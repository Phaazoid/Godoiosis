## The setting

Iosis is a **steampunk, low-fantasy** world: steam and primitive (mostly weaponized) electricity power airships, steam locomotives, and large clockwork mechanisms. Handheld steam weapons — **chainswords**, **springspears**, drills — are standard arms. Amputated limbs are replaced with **prosthetics** that wire into the nervous system and augment the limb with superhuman strength or durability.

All "magic" is **alchemy**: a mystic but scientifically rigorous tradition of understanding and transmuting the elements. Alchemists manipulate the elements through **runes** — stones inscribed with geometric symbols, each tied to an element or combination. Alchemy's original goal was the **Philosopher's Stone**, a legendary substance that could transmute freely and extend life.

## The fall, and what alchemy became

Society is still recovering from the collapse of a great empire centuries ago (the **Hermetic Empire** — see [../timeline.md](../timeline.md)). The people don't know why it fell, but alchemists remember it as a golden age when they wielded the Stone and unrivaled power — and they long to restore it.

The meaning of "alchemist" has since shifted — from *one who studies transmutation* to *one born with the inherited talent to use runes*. Modern alchemists descend from the ancients, who gained a **heritable affinity for alkahest** (the base element) through exposure to the Stone. Those born without it turned to technology to level the field, and are called **mechanists**.

When the Stones were destroyed, alchemists lost their power instantly — and the lower classes, long abused, turned on them. Alchemists were driven into hiding and persecution; war filled the vacuum, and a petty feudalism replaced the empire. As the rage cooled from witch-hunt to general suspicion, alchemists reassimilated but couldn't reclaim privilege. This split their motives — some researched how to make alchemy *useful to others* (to prove their worth), others hunted the Stone to *reclaim power*.

## The two great powers

Eventually alchemists rediscovered **runestone**, which — inscribed with symbols from ancient scrolls — let certain people (mostly those with affinities) manipulate elements again. The richest runestone lay in the ruined ancient capital, which became a Mecca for alchemists and, as their economic and military power grew, the seat of an alchemist state ruled by **five** elemental masters: **[Paracelsus](factions.md#paracelsus)**.

Other nations had rune-alchemists too, but depended on Paracelsus for runestone. As alchemists came to dominate the continent's military balance, rival kingdoms rallied and **pushed technological innovation to counter them** — driving the invention of new weapons and, ultimately, the mechanist **[Coalition Pax Facticius](factions.md#coalition-pax-facticius-cpf)**.
