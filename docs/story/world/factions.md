## Paracelsus

The alchemic capital of the known world — founded and run by alchemists, with an extremely **classist** society based on family status and individual alchemical prowess. Governed by an **oligarchy: the Council of Five**, the strongest alchemist of each element.

- The elite cluster in the **capital** 🏷 (unnamed; candidate "New Hermes") — a fortress-city of orderly, alchemically-built architecture with almost no crime or poverty *inside the walls*. Concern stops at those walls; poverty and crime are pushed to the borders, and the standard of living drops the farther you get from the center.
- The capital sits over the **ruins of the ancient city** where alchemic power originates — the source of most runes, with **mines reaching deep below the city** (a large battle in those mines could collapse parts of the capital; the city's **river** can be dammed and released for mayhem — see [Mechanist–Alchemist duality](../themes.md)).
- Holds a great **Library/archive** of runes and alchemy, open only to scholars and the elite.
- Stance toward the CPF: **disdain, not aggression** — Paracelsus sees mechanists as lesser and therefore not a threat. Its elite care only about preserving their power and unearthing the past. Its blind spot — ignorance of **alkahest** — and its hubris are framed as its eventual undoing.
- Place details: [../locations/paracelsus.md](../locations/paracelsus.md).

## Coalition Pax Facticius (CPF)

A **coalition of mechanist city-states** — "Factitious Peace" — united under **[King Campeador](../characters/npcs/king-campeador.md)**, who descends from the king of the largest founding city-state 🏷 that originally united them. The CPF exists to **balance Paracelsus's** once-unchecked military supremacy.

- Values are the inverse of Paracelsus's: alchemical ability is **not** a measure of standing. The CPF empowers its people through **technology**, which sparked a massive industrial revolution and an arms race to match elite alchemists. Not openly antagonistic toward Paracelsus, but wary and resentful of its contempt; it wants parity, not war.
- A confederation, so it has **several major city-states**, each with its own history, culture, and agenda: **[Aeroport](../locations/aeroport.md)**, the **[Great Scrap Heaps](../locations/scrap-heaps.md)** region, the (unnamed) capital, a plateau city, a coastal city, a mine city, and a slums region.
- Backstory threads: founded *after* alchemy was rediscovered (you can't define "non-alchemist" until alchemy exists), by smaller nations rallying for **safety and balance, not conquest**. Its sprawl-without-planning explains the airship-dependence between far-flung cities and the runaway industrial **waste** that created the Scrap Heaps. A small caste of high-status scientists monopolizes real innovation (and builds the war-machines/mechs you fight later), which has bred **secrecy and political corruption** — and deliberate technological ignorance in the big cities to hide what's going on.
- Place details: [../locations/cpf.md](../locations/cpf.md).

## The Bleeding Hearts

The mercenary band the player follows — formed by and around **[Torv](../characters/torv.md)**.

- Unlike mercenaries who chose the life for money or glory, the Bleeding Hearts exist as a **refuge for outcasts**: the poor, the persecuted, anyone "useful that he deems honest enough." Torv takes only jobs that align with his sense of justice, which is often impractical money-wise.
- **Outward mission:** give unwanted-but-honorable people a home and protect the weak. **Unspoken motive:** uncover and stop the forces behind the border **kidnappings** — Torv survived repeated Cartel assassination attempts as a loner and knew something bigger was in play.
- Base: a cave hideout near the wilderness, defensible with a single good earth alchemist. Roster: see [../characters/](../characters/).

## Ouroboros

A shadow society that **knows the lost secrets of the past** — including the Stone — and manipulates events from behind the scenes toward power and immortality.

- 🔴 Possibly a **defector splinter of the Philosopher's cult** that broke away *wanting* the Stone (the opposite of the Philosopher's goal). Its membership is small; it gets its muscle by **manipulating others** (the king, the Cartel) rather than acting directly → [Q1](../appendix/open-questions.md#q1-ouroboros-the-acolytes-and-the-cartel).
- **Method (the plot's throughline):** throw its enemies against each other to weaken them, and only step in at the very end, when one weakened group remains with its guard down.
- **Known/role-defined agents** (all unnamed — `Ouroboros Agents` doc: *"We need to name these guys"*):
  - The **king's "informant"** — feeds Campeador steered intelligence; the **first to recognize Isaac's alkahest**, and the one who turns the CPF's treatment of the Bleeding Hearts from near-arrest to heroes' welcome + Letter of Marque.
  - The **Cartel's handler** — feeds the Cartel Leader the method for fake stones and how to dodge Paracelsus's notice.
  - The **"diplomat"** — "miraculously" survives the airship crash, rides with the party through Part 2 steering them onto the Cartel, then fakes a heroic death to rejoin Ouroboros.
  - Possibly someone **high in the Paracelsus government — even one of the Council of Five.**
- The dragon-controlling agent who downs the airship (Part 1) implies Ouroboros has at least one **alchemically formidable** operative willing to act directly in a special case.

## The Philosopher's Acolytes

The **Philosopher's** secret society — **intentionally nameless**; its members are the "Acolytes."

- **Creed:** do not interfere with humanity directly; intervene only in matters relating to **alkahest**. Most members believe the Philosopher is still all-powerful.
- 🔴 In the reframed direction, only a few at the very top (if any) know he's been **weakened** for centuries — he sustains the myth with the "miracles" only he can still perform via alkahest. Cracks could show once the Bleeding Hearts prove the Acolytes aren't as omnipotent as they preach → [Q2](../appendix/open-questions.md#q2-the-philosophers-power-and-goal).
- Figurehead: [../characters/npcs/philosopher.md](../characters/npcs/philosopher.md).

## The Cartel 🏷

> **Working title only** — the dev dislikes it (*"no cartel calls themself 'the cartel'… we should find something better than a generic descriptor"*). Rename pending → [Names TBD](../README.md#names-tbd).

A **human-trafficking** operation that does the dirty work of manufacturing **fake Philosopher's Stones**.

- Born from Ouroboros's research into **aether**: living things give off aether when they die, so the Cartel captures and catalyzes that aether into a single crude, "half-working" but powerful form — a false stone that generates wealth and sustains shadow operations.
- Operates from an **underground favela in the outskirts of Paracelsus**. Wealthy from the fake stones, it hires as many morally-ambiguous merc gangs as it wants and stirs unrest along the borders where law enforcement is weak. It preys on people made alone by hardship — *those who won't be missed*. The public in the outskirts senses something is wrong (too many disappearances) but the Cartel exists only "in whispers."
- 🔴 Who really controls it — **Ouroboros**, the **Philosopher**, or a Leader who *thinks* he's independent — is the core of [Q1](../appendix/open-questions.md#q1-ouroboros-the-acolytes-and-the-cartel). See also [../characters/npcs/cartel-leader.md](../characters/npcs/cartel-leader.md).

## The Sultanate

A **faraway nation** with its own distinct alchemic tradition, home to **[Ali](../characters/ali.md)** (fire alchemist), **[Said](../characters/said.md)**, and **[Sara](../characters/sara.md)** (carbine mechanists). Loosely sketched — characterized so far by pride in its unique alchemy and a pilgrimage/adventure culture. Ali is rumored to be wanted for attempting to assassinate a **sultan** there.
