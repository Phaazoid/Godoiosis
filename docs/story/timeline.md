# Timeline / Pre-Game History

🟡 **Draft canon** · imported from `Story/Plot/World History/Pre-Game event timeline`, `Moderately Long Narrative Imperative`, and `Worldbuilding overview`.

All dates are "before the game's start." The numbers are deliberately soft in the source — treat the *sequence* as firmer than the exact years.

## Period 1 — the oldest of times (??? years ago)
Alchemy is first discovered. It is **born from humans, not man-made** — the first alchemists all carry an affinity for **alkahest** (chaos / the *prima materia*) and can use every kind of alchemy, though with limits.

## Period 2 — ~1000 years before the game
- **The Philosopher**, a genius of the age, discovers how to create the **Philosopher's Stone** (with a friend/collaborator). Stones are distributed to other alchemists. Wielders become god-like; the Hermetic Empire's rulers begin abusing the power and society destabilizes.
- Horrified at his creation, the Philosopher resolves to **wipe the Stones — and all who can make them — from history** to avert a calamity of his own making. He begins quietly: assassinations, so the wider community won't catch on, and founds his **cult** (the future [Acolytes](world/factions.md#the-philosophers-acolytes)) around this time.
- He triggers a chain reaction that **destroys all Stones nearly at once**; the Empire's ruling structure dissolves overnight. He and his followers then systematically hunt down everyone with an **alkahest** affinity — a hereditary trait, which forces him to kill **his own descendants**. 🔴 *Whether he spared a pregnant wife/line — and whether that's the root of Isaac's affinity — is an open thread* → [Q2](appendix/open-questions.md#q2-the-philosophers-power-and-goal).
- The remaining alchemists eventually realize what's happening; a **great battle** is fought on the land that will become Paracelsus. Its aftermath creates the largest **runestone** deposit and (in the reframed version) **greatly weakens the Philosopher**. 🏷 *This cataclysm needs a name.*
- Mostly successful, the weakened Philosopher fades from history. He takes the **last surviving Stone** and uses it for immortality, vowing to spend eternity ensuring no alkahest bloodline survived. His cult — believing him still all-powerful — becomes his eyes and ears, bound by a creed to **not interfere with humanity except in matters of alkahest**.

## Period 3 — ~700 years before the game
- The world is in a poor state. **"Modern" alchemy** is discovered: the marriage of *incomplete* (single-element) affinities with **runestone** — the alkahest "fallout" imbued into the ground by the great battle ~300 years earlier. See [world/alchemy-lore.md](world/alchemy-lore.md).
- **Paracelsus** is founded by alchemists over the ancient ruins/runestone; a new world power rises, ruled by a **Council of Five** (the strongest alchemist of each element). The Philosopher, alarmed at first, **loses interest** once he learns no true Stones can be made this way.
- Loose threads the source flags for this stretch: **CPF** history (newer than Paracelsus, though some founding states have older roots); the Philosopher growing **disillusioned with his own creed**; the formation of **[the Cartel](world/factions.md#the-cartel)** 🏷.

## Period 4 — just before the game
- **~100–50 years out:** the **runestone reserve runs low for the first time**; the fact is hushed up, and worry spreads among those who know.
- The mechanist nations rally into the **[Coalition Pax Facticius](world/factions.md#coalition-pax-facticius-cpf)** to balance Paracelsus's military supremacy, driving an industrial revolution and an arms race in new weapons. (Backstory in [locations/cpf.md](locations/cpf.md).)
- **~10 years out:** Isaac and Rebecca's parents — hiding their mother's alkahest affinity in poverty on the border — are finally tracked down and killed. The children (≈9–10) escape and fend for themselves. See [characters/npcs/dead-parents.md](characters/npcs/dead-parents.md).
- **Game start:** both siblings reach minimum enlistment age and travel to the Paracelsus Military Academy.

### Open consistency notes
- Two source docs tell the Period-2 events at different levels of detail and with slightly different framings (`Pre-Game event timeline` vs. `Moderately Long Narrative Imperative`). They agree on the spine; the richer narrative version adds the "spared wife / hidden from his own group" possibilities. Consolidated above; forks live in [open-questions.md](appendix/open-questions.md).
- The original alchemists are tied to the **Hermetic Empire** / **alkahest**; "modern" rune-alchemists are a later, weaker rediscovery. Keep that two-era distinction straight — it's load-bearing for the whole plot.
