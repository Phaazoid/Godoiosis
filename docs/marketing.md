## The one-sentence pitch

### Pitch language rules (dev-set, 2026-07-01)

- **Mechanist**, never machinist.
- **Alchemy**, never chemistry (or magic). Runes are **carved / inscribed**; the carving is a **transmutation**.
- "Clockwork" is doubly on-brand: determinism metaphor + steampunk flavor. Use it.

### What the pitch must carry (dev direction, 2026-07-01)

1. **Clockwork squad planning** — queue the whole squad's turn, watch it resolve.
2. **Two layers**: an alchemy layer and a steampunk (mechanist) layer.
3. **Deep customization** of both weapons and runes (carve your own transmutations; author weapon variants).

Determinism rides implicitly on "clockwork"; it can return explicitly in a second sentence. Round-1 hooks (death system, elemental reactions) demote to supporting copy.

### Round 2 drafts (2026-07-01)

1. Iosis is a tactical RPG where you carve your own runes, engineer your own weapons, and wind your squads up like clockwork.
2. Iosis is a tactical RPG where you queue your whole squad's turn and watch it resolve like clockwork — fighting with weapons you engineered and runes you carved yourself.
3. Iosis is a tactical RPG where two crafts shape every soldier — mechanist steel and alchemic runecraft — and every turn is a clockwork plan you wind up and watch unfold.
4. Iosis is a tactical RPG of mechanist machines and carved alchemic runes, where you build each soldier's kit yourself, queue the squad's whole turn, and watch the plan tick through.
5. Iosis is a tactical RPG where alchemists inscribe their spells onto runestone, mechanists build theirs out of springs and chainblades — and you plan every squad's turn like winding a watch.

**Current lean:** #1 as the one-liner (three verbs = three layers: carve/engineer/wind), #5 as the expanded Steam-page opener.

### Round 1 drafts (superseded — kept for phrase salvage)

- "The 95% never misses" — determinism wink, usable in body copy.
- *"A death is never a surprise, only a consequence"* (from `design/philosophy.md` Axiom 3) — killer second-sentence candidate.
- "They fall, lose limbs, and fight on rebuilt, until you start to wonder if they're still the same person" — death-system/Ship-of-Theseus copy for story-leaning audiences.
- "Freeze the river, march your squad across, melt it behind them" — pairs with the fire/ice GIF.

**Test for any draft:** would it work as the first line of a Steam page? As a Reddit post title?
