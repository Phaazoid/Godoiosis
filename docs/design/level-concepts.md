# Level Concepts — Set-Piece Idea Pool

**Status: IDEA POOL (workshop) — filed 2026-07-04** from a synthesis session (story docs × design docs). These are *potential* levels, not a commitment or an ordering. Sibling to [../story/locations/battle-locales.md](../story/locations/battle-locales.md) (the raw locale brainstorm); this doc adds the systems-pairing: which mechanics each set piece showcases.

> **Pacing principle (dev, 2026-07-04): showcase ≠ introduction.** Late levels may be the *showcase* for a system (Will, taming, weather…), but they must not be its *first appearance* — otherwise half the game passes without the toolbox. Introduce each mechanic early in an ordinary level, test it in a mixed level, then let the set piece be the graduation exam. (Teach → test → twist.)

## Centipede tank boss — squad-splitting design (dev, 2026-07-04)

Answers battle-locales' open question (*"destroy a middle segment — split or contract?"*): **split, and the split is the mechanic.**

- The whole tank is **one squad**; each segment a unit. Attacking it from the ends means engaging a giant intact party — counter rules C1–C7 make every segment's response available, all day.
- **Destroy a middle joint → the halves become two squads** (mechanically: member removal + leader reassignment + cohesion-range detach — close to what I5/I6 already do). Two smaller parties = far less counter exposure per engagement.
- "Hit it from the ends and fight a giant squad all day; hit the middle and divide-and-conquer." The boss *teaches why squads are strong by making you fight one* — squad-splitting as boss counterplay is the squad system's thesis inverted.
